## 4. ⚠️ ASSUMPTIONS — MUST REVIEW WITH MAX & TIZ

> Every number in this document rests on the assumptions below. None have been validated by market research or customer interviews. All must be stress-tested before the document is used in any investor or grant context.

### A. Pricing Assumptions

- [ ] **HET Unit ASP €80,000** — Based on publicly cited ranges for 200W-class thrusters (Sitael HT100, Bradford ECAPS HPGP comparables). Actual market pricing is opaque. Tiz to validate against known procurement budgets (ESA CDF studies, GSTP contracts).
- [ ] **Software licence €15,000/year** — Purely notional. No customer discovery has been done. Could be lower (€5–10k) for early adopters or higher (€30k+) for institutional customers (primes, agencies).
- [ ] **Services engagement €30,000** — Assumes 3–5 person-weeks of effort per engagement. Needs validation against Max's consulting rate and Tiz's billable hours estimate.

### B. Volume / Sales Assumptions

- [ ] **Year 1: zero thruster sales** — Assumed pre-revenue R&D year. Is there any LOI or letter of intent from a potential anchor customer that could accelerate this?
- [ ] **Year 2: 1 thruster unit** — This assumes a single demo/pilot sale (potentially subsidised or at cost). Who is the target customer? NewSpace operator? Research institute? University nanosatellite programme?
- [ ] **Year 3: 3 units, Year 4: 8 units, Year 5: 15 units** — Growth rate implies significant market penetration in VLEO constellation segment. No market size analysis has been done to validate addressable unit volume. Compare against: how many VLEO satellites are being built per year?
- [ ] **Software licence growth (3 → 8 → 15 → 25 customers)** — Assumes Aegis RL has standalone value to customers who do NOT buy a thruster (e.g. operators with legacy propulsion wanting simulation tools). This is a separate product hypothesis that needs validation.
- [ ] **Services engagements (1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5/year)** — Assumes consulting pipeline grows linearly. Is there enough demand for HET simulation consulting specifically, or does this need to broaden to general electric propulsion (EP) consulting?

### C. Cost Assumptions

- [ ] **Hardware COGS 40%** — Industry rule-of-thumb for space hardware. Actual BOM cost for a 200W HET (discharge chamber, magnetic circuit, PPU) needs a real cost model. Tiz to produce rough BOM estimate.
- [ ] **Software COGS 10%** — Assumes primarily cloud hosting and support costs. Low but defensible for a SaaS model. Needs review once hosting architecture is decided.
- [ ] **Services COGS 25%** — Assumes founders deliver most consulting personally in early years (low direct cost). Rises as junior staff are billed. Reasonable but optimistic.
- [ ] **Prototyping budget Year 1: €150k** — Covers: vacuum-compatible hardware components, PPU prototyping, propellant handling (Krypton). Needs a bottom-up build from Tiz's technical plan.
- [ ] **Prototyping budget Year 2: €200k** — Higher than Year 1 due to full integrated system build. To be validated.

### D. Personnel Assumptions

- [ ] **2 founders Year 1 at €120k total (€60k each)** — This is below-market for experienced engineers in Western Europe but may be appropriate for an early-stage Estonian company drawing ESA BIC support. Founders to agree on actual draw.
- [ ] **Headcount plan (4 → 7 → 10 → 13)** — Hiring plan roles not yet defined. Suggested sequence: Year 2 adds propulsion test engineer + software engineer; Year 3 adds business development + systems engineer; Years 4–5 add manufacturing/production staff. Needs explicit role definitions.
- [ ] **€70k average salary per head (implied)** — This is the implied average (€910k / 13 heads in Year 5). Reasonable for Estonia/remote-first team but needs validation against local salary benchmarks (e.g. Estonian tax board data, Glassdoor Tallinn).

### E. Market & Competitive Assumptions

- [ ] **VLEO market size** — Model assumes sufficient customer base exists. Reference: ESA VLEO study (2022), Skimsat, Destinus, D-Orbit as potential customers. No signed MOUs exist.
- [ ] **Krypton propellant supply chain** — Krypton availability post-2022 (Ukraine conflict impact on noble gas supply) is a real risk. Tiz to assess alternative propellant options (Xenon, Iodine) and their impact on thruster design and ASP.
- [ ] **AI/ML differentiation (Aegis RL)** — The model assumes Aegis RL commands a premium over dumb thrusters. This must be quantified: what efficiency gain (Isp improvement, propellant saving) does the AI provide, and what is that worth in €/mission to an operator?
- [ ] **IP / patent strategy** — No IP budget is included. Patent filings for the magnetic shielding AI control algorithm should be costed (est. €15–30k per patent family including PCT). Add to G&A or R&D line.

### F. Funding Assumptions

- [ ] **ESA BIC grant quantum €50–100k** — Verify current ESA BIC Estonia programme terms. Some tranches are milestone-gated and in-kind (facility access, mentoring) rather than cash.
- [ ] **Angel close at €650–700k** — No investor conversations have been had. Timeline to close (typically 6–12 months for pre-seed hardware) should be factored into runway planning.
- [ ] **Seed at €1.5–2M in Year 2** — Assumes M1–M7 are achieved and the company has a bankable track record. Hardware seed rounds in Europe are hard; consider ESA GSTP, EIC Accelerator, national programmes (Enterprise Estonia) as alternatives or bridges.

### G. Macro / Structural Assumptions

- [ ] **No revenue from ESA contracts in Year 1–2** — Some space startups generate revenue via ESA STAR and GSTP study contracts before product sales. This could add €50–200k/year in Year 2–3 and de-risks the model significantly. Worth pursuing in parallel.
- [ ] **Break-even at Year 5** — The model is highly sensitive to thruster unit sales. Selling 12 units instead of 15 in Year 5 pushes break-even to Year 6+. A sensitivity table should be built for the pitch deck.
- [ ] **No depreciation of IP or internally generated intangibles** — D&A is estimated at €20k/year (physical assets only). If Aegis RL is capitalised as an intangible asset under IFRS for SMEs, amortisation would increase.
- [ ] **Exchange rate / currency** — Model is in EUR. If components are sourced in USD (US suppliers), FX risk on COGS exists. Not modelled.
