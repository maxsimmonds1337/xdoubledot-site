The Technical Challenge

Cathode Erosion: The Silent Killer of Hall Thrusters

Every Hall Effect Thruster depends on a cathode to generate electrons for plasma ionization. But high-energy ion bombardment causes progressive cathode erosion, limiting thruster lifespan to 10,000-15,000 hours.

The Erosion Cycle

Ion bombardment strips material from the cathode surface. As geometry changes, plasma becomes unstable. Efficiency drops. The thruster fails.

Industry Standard: Accept & Replace

Current solutions treat erosion as inevitable. Satellites carry redundant thrusters or plan for shorter missions. This adds mass, cost, and risk.

Cathode Erosion Impact

Mission Life Limited 2-3 years
Efficiency Loss Over Time 15-25%

ABHEP: Air-Breathing Propulsion

The "Holy Grail" of VLEO. Utilising residual atmosphere as fuel for dramatically extended mission lifetimes. Our magnetic shielding addresses the primary life-limiting factor: reactive atmospheric ions accelerate cathode erosion faster than any noble gas.

Why Air-Breathing Needs Our Tech

Atmospheric gases (N2, O2) are highly reactive and dramatically accelerate cathode erosion. Traditional HETs fail within hours.

Our AI-guided magnetic shielding directly addresses the hardest problem in air-breathing propulsion: keeping the cathode alive long enough to matter. We're not just building thrusters — we're building the platform that makes persistent VLEO viable.
